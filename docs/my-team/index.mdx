# Your Team Overview

Your Team Overview is the starting point for checking roster context, minute management, and the current training week. In the app, this surface is labeled My Team.

## What this page is for

Use this page when you need a fast read on how your own team is doing right now:

- confirm that the roster and lineup data are synced,
- compare player usage in the Lineup Stats tab,
- review current-week training minutes and the selected training target,
- check Game Shape coverage for minute management, and
- decide whether the next question belongs in tactical history, a player page, the schedule, or a match analysis page.

## When to use each page

| Surface | App route | Use it when | Best question |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Your Team overview | `/team` | You want the roster-level summary with Lineup Stats, Training Minutes, and Game Shape in one place. | “Is the team data current, and which tab should I open next?” |
| Your Team tactical history | `/team/tactics` | You want patterns across imported games instead of a single-game readout. | “How has our team actually played across multiple games?” |
| Player pages | `/player/[id]/overview`, `/player/[id]/history` | You want one player's fit, skills, recent games, or development history. | “How is this player trending?” |
| Schedule | `/schedule` | You want the season view, upcoming games, or a shortcut into a specific match. | “What is coming next?” |
| Match analysis | `/match/[id]` | You want one completed game, its boxscore, and the imported evidence behind it. | “What happened in that game?” |

## How to read it

The page is split into three tabs.

### Lineup Stats

Lineup Stats is the roster-and-match view. It can show season aggregate data or a single selected match. Use the match filter when you want to understand one game in context; use the season aggregate when you want a broader baseline.

When the roster has not been synced, the page shows a clear empty state instead of guessing. If a roster snapshot is available, the table shows its date so you can tell how fresh the underlying skills data is.

### Training Minutes

Training Minutes is the current-week planner view. Choose the training area and the specific option, then review the minute target and the list of eligible matches that were counted.

Two details matter here:

- the chosen training area and option are remembered locally in your browser, not saved as a server-side assignment, and
- the included and excluded match audit shows which games were counted for the current training week.

If no eligible games have been recorded yet, the page says so directly. That is a timing issue, not a failure of the planner.

### Game Shape

Game Shape tracks the current week’s minute coverage. It is a workload check, not a player-rating page.

The warning text on this tab matters: zero-minute values are current-week coverage, not a signal that the player has become weak. Use it to spot overuse, underuse, or missing minutes before you over-interpret the numbers.

## Guided workflow

1. Open `/team` and confirm that the roster and current sync state look reasonable.
2. Start with Lineup Stats if you need a season baseline or want to inspect one match from the search list.
3. Switch to Training Minutes when you need to verify the current training target, the minute target, and which games counted.
4. Switch to Game Shape when you want a minute-management check for the same roster.
5. Move to `/team/tactics` when you need repeating tactical patterns, `/player/[id]/overview` or `/player/[id]/history` when the question is player-specific, `/schedule` when the question is about upcoming games, and `/match/[id]` when the question is about one completed game.

## Data and freshness

This page is built from synced JerryBB team, roster, match, boxscore, and training data. The page reflects whatever has already been imported; it can lag behind BuzzerBeater until you refresh the team or import the missing games.

The current-week views use the eligible matches that fall inside the training week window, so the numbers can change after the next import or sync.

## Limitations and confidence

- Empty states mean the app does not yet have enough synced data for that section.
- Season aggregate and single-match views answer different questions, so do not compare them as if they were the same sample.
- Game Shape coverage is not a player rating.
- Tactical history and match analysis are separate surfaces; do not use one as a substitute for the other.
- I could not capture a live contextual screenshot in this session because the production app was behind the member-access gate.
