# Opponent Tactical History

Opponent Tactical History examines how an opponent has behaved across multiple games. It is designed for pattern recognition and context, not as a replacement for the matchup-specific briefing.

## What this page is for

Use this page when you want to understand whether an opponent's tactical behavior is recurring, how often different tactics appear, and how those choices have performed in the available history.

## Decisions supported

It can help you investigate:

- Does the opponent repeatedly favor a particular tactic?
- Are observed tactics frequent, effective, or both?
- Is the history broad enough to support a meaningful conclusion?
- Which patterns should be carried into the upcoming briefing for closer matchup review?

## How to interpret the history

Keep three things separate:

- **Observed behavior:** what tactics appeared.
- **Calculated performance:** how outcomes were summarized.
- **Recommendation:** what JerryBB suggests considering next.

A tactic appearing frequently is not automatically a good tactic. Consider the time window, games included, sample size, opponent strength, and completeness of the underlying data.

## Difference from the briefing

The briefing focuses on one upcoming game. Tactical History looks across games to identify patterns. Historical evidence informs preparation, but matchup-specific context still matters.
